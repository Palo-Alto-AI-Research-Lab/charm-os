# C(H+A)RM: CRM for Humans + Agents

## The name

**C(H+A)RM** = **C**RM for **H**umans **+ A**gents **R**elationship **M**anagement.

It is memorable, and yes, it reads like "charm". That is fine as a headline, but we never rely on the acronym alone. Every use is paired with a plain-language line:

> **C(H+A)RM is the first open-source CRM that manages both your human contacts and your AI agents as first-class relationships.**

## Why a new category

A CRM has always assumed the other party is human. That assumption is breaking. You now work with AI agents that have:

- a **history** (what they did, how well),
- a **role** in your network (researcher, drafter, scheduler),
- **reliability** worth tracking,
- **context** worth maintaining.

Treating an agent as a contact, with a profile, notes, and follow-ups, is not a metaphor. It is how you actually keep a growing fleet of agents useful.

## Relation to existing terms

- **Personal CRM / PRM** (e.g. Monica): humans only, no AI.
- **Relationship intelligence** (e.g. proprietary tools): auto-capture and analytics for human networks, not agent-aware, not open source.
- **Agent memory** (e.g. Mem0, Letta): stores facts for agents, but does not model the agent as a relationship you manage, and has no human CRM.

C(H+A)RM is the union: **relationship intelligence extended to include AI agents, open source, grounded in your own second brain.**

## Honest framing

This is an uncharted category. Adoption depends on whether people accept that an AI agent deserves the same management as a human contact. We argue it does, and we back the claim with working tooling rather than slogans.
