# Manifesto

## The problem

Your knowledge, your AI agents' memory, and your relationships live in three different tools that never talk to each other. Your notes do not know who you know. Your CRM does not know what you researched. Your AI assistant forgets both the moment a session ends. Every important decision starts half-blind.

## The thesis

One owned system should hold all three, and a single loop should turn them into decisions.

### 1. A second brain you own

Local-first markdown, semantic recall (embeddings + reranker), and an always-on memory ledger that records every working turn at near-zero cost. The goal is not "search". The goal is a growing model of how you think, a digital twin that outlives any single chat session or vendor.

### 2. Agents are relationships too

A CRM has always been for humans. But you now work alongside AI agents that have history, context, reliability, and a role in your network. C(H+A)RM treats a human contact and an AI agent with the same primitives: a profile, a history, notes, and follow-ups. Managing your agents is as real as managing your people.

### 3. RDR: the decision loop

No strategic move on memory alone. The loop is:

- **Recall** to pull everything you already know from your second brain.
- **Deep Research** to find what you do not know yet, from the outside world.
- **Synthesis** to merge both into a short Decision Memo, then act.

Recall is necessary but never sufficient. Deep Research without recall repeats old mistakes. The loop is the product.

## Principles

- **You own it.** Local-first, your data never leaves your machine unless you send it.
- **Repairable by a human.** Simple enough that a non-engineer can understand and fix it. Complexity must justify itself.
- **Code out, data home.** We open-source the framework, never the personal data. Examples are always synthetic.
- **Evidence over slogans.** New ideas (the C(H+A)RM category, the RDR loop) are backed by working tooling and documented practice, not buzzwords.

## North star

A digital twin: a system that thinks, advises, and decides the way you do, and keeps doing it as you grow. This framework is the scaffolding for that twin, shared openly so others can build their own.
